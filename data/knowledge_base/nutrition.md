# Nutrition and Macros

Practical, general nutrition guidance for training. Individual needs vary; a
dietitian can personalise this.

## Calories and Body Composition

Body weight change is driven by energy balance over time. A modest surplus
(roughly 250–500 kcal above maintenance) supports muscle gain with limited fat;
a modest deficit (roughly 300–500 kcal below) drives fat loss while preserving
muscle if protein and training stay high. Track trends over weeks, not daily
scale noise.

## Protein

Protein is the priority macro for lifters. Around 1.6–2.2 g per kg of bodyweight
per day supports muscle growth and recovery. Spreading it across 3–4 meals of
20–40 g is a reasonable structure. Sources: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes,
tofu, and powders.

## Carbohydrates and Fats

Carbohydrates fuel hard training and recovery — keep them around your workouts if
performance dips. Fats support hormones; do not drop them too low (roughly 0.5–1
g per kg per day is a sensible floor). Once protein and calories are set, carb and
fat split is largely preference.

## Hydration and Practicality

Drink to thirst and keep urine pale. The boring fundamentals — enough protein,
enough total calories for your goal, mostly whole foods, consistency — beat any
clever hack. Perfect diets you cannot sustain lose to good diets you can.
