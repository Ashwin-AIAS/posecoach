# Supplements That Actually Have Evidence

General educational information only — not medical or dosing advice. Check with a
doctor or pharmacist before starting a supplement, especially with medications or
health conditions.

## Creatine Monohydrate

The most studied sports supplement. Around 3–5 g per day of creatine monohydrate
supports strength and muscle gains over time. A loading phase is optional. It
draws a little water into muscle, which is normal. It is well tolerated in healthy
people; those with kidney conditions should ask a doctor first.

## Protein Powder

Whey or plant protein is a convenient way to hit a daily protein target, not a
magic muscle builder. It is food in powder form. If you already reach your protein
goal from meals, a powder is optional. Total daily protein matters far more than
timing.

## Caffeine

A reliable pre-workout stimulant that can improve focus and perceived effort,
roughly 3–6 mg per kg bodyweight taken 30–60 minutes before training. Avoid late
in the day so it does not wreck sleep, and do not stack multiple high-dose
products.

## What To Be Skeptical Of

Most fat burners, testosterone boosters, and exotic "anabolic" blends have weak or
no evidence. Save your money for food, sleep, and training consistency. Beware
proprietary blends that hide doses, and remember supplements are lightly
regulated — third-party tested products are safer.
