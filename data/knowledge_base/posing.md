# Bodybuilding Posing Guide

Educational reference for the mandatory poses judged across bodybuilding and
physique divisions, and the common faults a coach watches for. PoseCoach scores
the *geometry* of a pose — limb position, left/right symmetry, and how steadily
you hold it. It cannot see muscularity, conditioning, separation, or how hard you
are flexing, which are a large part of real judging. Treat the cues here as setup
and alignment guidance, not a measure of physique quality.

## Front Double Biceps

A front-facing mandatory in Open Bodybuilding, Classic Physique, and Women's
Physique. Face the judges, raise both arms to roughly shoulder height or slightly
above, and curl the fists in so the biceps peak. The forearms should sit close to
vertical and the elbows level with or just above the shoulder line. Keep the
stance about shoulder width with the knees pushed out and the quads flexed.
PoseCoach checks elbow height relative to the shoulders, forearm angle, fist
position, stance width, and left/right symmetry of the arms.

## Front Lat Spread

A front mandatory showing the V-taper. Face the judges, place the hands on the
lower lats or waist, and flare the lats outward so the upper back widens. The arms
stay near-straight and roughly parallel to the ground, with the elbows close to
shoulder height. Drive the lats wide rather than shrugging the shoulders up.
PoseCoach checks that the upper arms are close to horizontal, the elbows sit near
the shoulder line, and the left and right sides match.

## Rear Double Biceps

The double-biceps geometry seen from behind. Turn your back to the judges and hit
the same arm position as the front double biceps — elbows up, forearms vertical,
fists curled. Place one foot back with the heel raised onto the toes to bring out
the rear calf and hamstring. Because this is a rear frontal view, left/right
symmetry is still meaningful and is scored. PoseCoach checks arm height, forearm
angle, the raised-heel gap between the ankles, and symmetry.

## Rear Lat Spread

A rear mandatory showing back width. Back to the judges, hands on the waist or
lower lats, elbows flared forward, and the lats spread as wide as possible. As
with the rear double biceps, one heel is usually raised to set the rear leg.
PoseCoach checks elbow flare, hand-to-hip position, stance width, and left/right
symmetry of the elbows and hands.

## Side Chest and Side Triceps

Profile poses. In the side chest you stand side-on, bend the front knee and raise
the front heel, keep the back leg straight, and clasp the hands to contract the
chest and arm facing the judges. In the side triceps the front upper arm pins to
the side while the rear hand reaches toward the small of the back, again with the
front leg flexed and heel lifted. Because half the body is occluded in profile,
left/right symmetry is NOT scored for side poses — PoseCoach scores only the
profile-valid checks such as front-knee flexion and the raised heel.

## Abdominal and Thigh

A front mandatory. Face the judges, place both hands behind the head with the
elbows flared, crunch the abdominals, and extend one thigh forward to show the
quad. The classic fault is forgetting to actually contract the abs while focusing
on the legs. PoseCoach checks the hands-behind-head position, elbow flare, and
that one thigh is extended.

## Most Muscular

A front "crab" pose in Open Bodybuilding. Clasp the hands together at the centre
of the torso, roll the shoulders forward, and contract everything at once. There
are several variants (hands-clasped, hammer), but all share the fists-together,
shoulders-forward shape. PoseCoach checks that the wrists are together near the
mid-torso and the shoulders are hunched forward.

## Men's Physique Quarter Turns

Men's Physique is judged in board shorts through quarter turns rather than the
bodybuilding mandatories. The distinguishing rule is that one hand rests on the
hip during the side and rear turns. You rotate front, to the side, to the rear,
and to the other side, presenting a relaxed, confident line at each stop with feet
about hip width. PoseCoach verifies the orientation at each quarter turn and the
hand-on-hip position.

## Women's Divisions: Bikini, Wellness, Figure

Bikini, Wellness, and Figure use the same quarter-turn framework as Men's
Physique. Bikini and Wellness emphasise a relaxed, confident front and back
presentation; Figure adds hands-on-hips front and back turns to display the
V-taper. Wellness places extra emphasis on lower-body development in the rear
turn. PoseCoach scores the quarter-turn orientation and the hand and stance
positions that each division calls for.

## Common Posing Faults

Across divisions the recurring faults are: arms drifting below shoulder height in
biceps shots, forearms angled rather than vertical, an uneven shoulder line or one
elbow higher than the other (a symmetry fault), a stance that is too narrow or too
wide, forgetting to raise the front heel in side and rear poses, and shaking or
drifting out of the pose instead of holding it still. Practising each pose in
front of a mirror or camera and holding it for several seconds builds the
steadiness that judges reward.

## Holding and Transitions

Judging happens over held poses and the transitions between them. Hit each pose
cleanly, hold it steady for several seconds without swaying, and move smoothly to
the next without fidgeting. A pose held with control reads far better than one
that is rushed or shaky. PoseCoach measures hold steadiness as the temporal
stability of your keypoints — the less you drift, the higher the steadiness score.
